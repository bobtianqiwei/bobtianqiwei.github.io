DESIGN & ENGINEERING

1
Sympathetic Orchestra
Interactive design, Individual project (non-academic).
The goal is to conduct a virtual orchestra on a computer.
Inspired by my experience learning to conduct, I recognize gestures with Leap Motion, write interactive pages and programs with Processing (Java) to process information and provide visual feedback, process audios in each part with Logic Pro, and produce music with real-time feedback from speakers or headphones.
2
Emotionally Intelligent Flower Raising Device
Product design, Individual project (academic).
The topic is smart home products, which requires design solutions and models.
My concept of design is for plants to live and interact with people in their homes, like pets.
Research of market and users was conducted in the early stage; Functional models were developed with Raspberry Pi and various sensors; 3D models were built using Rhino, and image and video rendering was done with KeyShot.
3
Digital Paradise
Interactive Arts, Individual Project (Non-Academic).
I hope to use virtual scenes to trigger the audience’s reflection on social phenomena.
Virtual scenes with interactive features were created using Unity.
4
A Fish in The Northern Ocean（北冥有鱼）
Mechanical devices, group project(academic). My job: Design and assembly of The Ocean, a musical mechanism.
Assignment of the sophomore mechanical design class, topic: musical installation.
I proposed the design concept of the whole installation and led the group in completing the production of the entire installation. The installation plays Philip Glass’s Opening while a Peng dances to the music. The mechanical mechanism was designed according to the sheet music; the mechanical structure was designed with the aid of Fusion 360; the drawings were drawn by Auto CAD, and the mechanical structure was made using laser-cut wood and acrylic plates, Lego Mindstorms.
5
Xiao In: Parcel Sterilization Helper
Product design and prototyping, group project(academic). My job: product concept formulation, mechanical design, assembly and commissioning of prototypes.
Assignment of Robotics Development, topic: intelligent robots.
User research was conducted in the early stage; the entire prototype was developed using Arduino, mechanical design and drawing was carried out with SolidWorks, Auto CAD, and the servo and water pump were mounted on the body and robotic arms, which were made of laser-cut aluminum plates.

Instruments for VR music games

Assignment of Product Design course, topic: entertainment products.
3D modeling and design was conducted using Rhino, image rendering with KeyShot, and design solutions were complete.

8
Rachmaninoff: Piano Concerto No.2 in C Minor, Op.18: I.Moderato
Tianqi Wei. Tsinghua University Student Art Troupe & Shengfan Cao
Piano concerto, live performance. My job: conducting.
I organized all the rehearsals, planned the whole show, and took charge of the recording and mixing. I designed the treatment of musical expression based on the orchestra’s setting and utilized the characteristics of a small orchestra to imbue my music with a level of details not found in a large ensemble. I designed the staging requirements based on the concert hall’s soundstage structure and used my self-taught knowledge of recording and mixing to design the placement of recording equipment so that the orchestra’s sound was delicately balanced for both the audience’s ears and the recording equipment in a straightforward manner.

Tianqi Wei

12
J.S.Bach: Concerto in D Minor, BWV 974: II. Adagio
Tianqi Wei
Piano solo, live performance.
I try to understand this adaptation of the Baroque oboe concerto from the point of view of a clavier instrument. While retaining the relationship between the voices and the composition structure of the original concerto, it highlights the characteristics of the piano as a keyboard instrument, adding more ruggedness to the music.
